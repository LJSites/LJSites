§ 03 · Questions worth asking

The fine print, up front.

Common questions before signing on. If yours isn't here, email us — we'll add it.

Why isn't the back-end priced openly?
Because every back-end project is different. One restaurant needs online ordering and reservations. Another needs a loyalty program and gift cards. Pricing back-end work off a brochure means you either over-pay for things you don't use, or under-quote and end up cutting corners. We'd rather have a 30-minute call, scope it carefully, and send you a fixed quote.
Is there a contract or commitment?
No long-term contract. The front-end monthly is month-to-month — cancel any month and the site is still yours. Back-end projects are fixed-fee, paid in milestones. We don't have lock-ins.
What does the $200 monthly cover?
Hosting on a fast CDN, SSL, your custom domain, monthly content updates (hours, photos, copy, new menu items), analytics, the contact form, security patches, and us being on call. No hourly meter, no nickel-and-diming.
Can I start front-end and add back-end later?
That's how most of our long-running projects start. Get the front-end live first, learn what your customers actually do, then we build the back-end functionality that addresses what you saw. No need to figure it all out up front.
What if I just want the site, no monthly?
Possible. We can hand you the HTML file, set up a domain, and walk away. The site is yours — we just won't be available for the monthly updates, the analytics dashboards, or the on-call support. Price is the same one-time setup fee.
Do you do e-commerce?
Yes — that falls under the back-end plan. From a simple Stripe checkout for one product to a full catalog with inventory, variants, shipping, and abandoned-cart automation. Scoped to fit how your business actually sells.
How quickly do updates land?
Small content edits (hours, copy, swap a photo) usually ship the same business day. Bigger changes (new section, new design element) typically within the week. Back-end changes follow whatever release schedule we agree on at scoping.
What if I'm not in LA?
We're set up for the Greater LA area but we've taken on a handful of remote projects. If you're farther away, email us and we'll tell you honestly whether it's a good fit.
