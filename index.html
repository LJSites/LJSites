§ How it works · README.md
README.md
updated · last week

A pipeline, not a sales funnel.

Every LJ Sites mockup is a single self-contained HTML file: hand-tuned typography, real photos pulled live from Google Places, an embedded map, and a call button. We build it speculatively — you only pay if you decide to keep it.

1discover

Find the business

We sweep the LA area for small businesses with no website — or only a Facebook page. Real places that deserve a real front door.

2design

Build the mockup

Photos, hours, menu, testimonies — all real, all hand-set. One archetype per site, picked to fit the place. No templates.

3ship

Publish to the web

Within minutes the mockup is live on a clean ljsites.github.io URL. We hand it to the owner. They can call themselves from it.

4handover

It's theirs

Flat fee, no monthly. We transfer the repo, point the domain, and stay on call for the first month. Otherwise it stays online as our portfolio.

§ About · /ljsites

A two-person studio
in Pasadena.

We kept walking into restaurants and shops we loved that didn't show up on Google. We got tired of that.

So we built a pipeline: find local places without a real online front door, ship them a single-page mockup as a sales pitch, and let the work speak for itself. No cold outreach, no pitch decks, no contracts to sign before you've seen what we'd actually make for you.

Every site is hand-tuned. We don't ship templates. We don't take retainers. If you decide to keep it, we transfer the repo and disappear.
